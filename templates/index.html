COLORFUL IMAGE COLORIZATION

A study by Richard Zhang, Phillip Isola, Alexei A. Efros (2018)

Given a grayscale photograph as input, Zhang et al. (2018) addressed the issue of hallucinating a plausible color version of the photograph. They proposed a fully automatic approach that produces vibrant and realistic colorizations by posing it as a classification task. Their method shows that colorization can be a powerful pretext task for self-supervised feature learning, acting as a cross-channel encoder. This approach results in state-of-the-art performance on several feature learning benchmarks.
